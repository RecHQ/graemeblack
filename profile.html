After graduating in fashion at Edinburgh College of Art in 1989, Graeme Black began his career in London as an Assistant Designer for John Galliano, Arabella Pollen and Zandra Rhodes among others. A move to Italy in 1993 saw him work as a designer for Les Copains and in 1995 he moved on and through the ranks at Giorgio Armani to become Head Designer at Borgonuovo Black Label. In 2003 Black became the Creative Consultant for Ferragamo and during his time there he had responsibility for the co-ordination of all categories of womenswear and accessories and staged the biannual fashion show in Milan, as well as one-off events in the US and Far East.

2005 saw the launch of the first Graeme Black collection and in September 2007 Graeme left Ferragamo to concentrate on his own line. The company was based in Italy with a showroom in Milan and production office in Florence. In 2007 his London design studio was opened and participated in London Fashion Week, whilst sales continued to be conducted in both Milan and Paris. The line was sold internationally to stores including Saks Fifth Avenue and Neiman Marcus in the USA; Browns and Harrods in UK; Lane Crawford in Hong Kong and many other diverse multi-brand boutiques.

In 2009 Graeme Black developed a consultancy business based in London aimed at the luxury market. Specialising in collection development, fashion strategy and trend forecasting through to branding and store development, his clients to date include Hugo Boss (Germany), Desa (Turkey) and Les Copains (Italy).

In 2011 Graeme was appointed as Creative Director of 1436 (Beijing) - the Chinese luxury cashmere brand. 1436 is renowned for its expertise in creating the finest cashmere of the highest quality from the Etuoke grasslands of Inner Mongolia. To date, the brand has established more than 42 stores throughout China; most recently opening its flagship store, designed by renowned American architect Michael Gabbellini, in Beijing. Graeme has regularly shot both mens and womens campaigns in New York with well-known photographers such as Walter Chin and Sebastian Kim. Co-ordinating creatively with his team from design studio to showroom and campaign to catalogue.
